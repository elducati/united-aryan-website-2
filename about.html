Growth Story

Our Journey

2002
Founded — Unit 1

The company began operations with Unit 1, featuring 17 full production lines — establishing a strong foothold in Kenya's Export Processing Zone.

Mid 2006
Expansion — Unit 2

Capacity was nearly doubled with the commissioning of Unit 2, bringing total production capacity to 38 lines to meet growing international demand.

Today
5,000+ Employees & Growing

With over 5,000 direct employees and ambitious plans for vertical integration, United Aryan continues to scale while deepening its positive impact on the Kenyan community.

Looking Ahead

Future Plans

We are actively working on a vertical integration programme to bring more manufacturing steps in-house — reducing lead times and strengthening quality control.

Textile Mill

A yarn-to-finished Denim fabric plant with 2 million units per month capacity, planned once the US market stabilises — all numbers and details already worked out.

Dyeing & Winding Unit

Color threads are a critical raw material for Kenya's garment industry. We plan an in-house dyeing and winding unit to control quality and cost.

Ancillary Manufacturing

Moulding hangers, manufacturing cartons, and producing woven and printed labels in-house — reducing dependency on external suppliers.

Our Mission

"The intention behind this business is to create as many jobs as possible. Our goal is to have at least 10,000 direct jobs created in the near future — meaning 100,000 people get meals every day. In Africa, one direct job supports 10 dependents."
